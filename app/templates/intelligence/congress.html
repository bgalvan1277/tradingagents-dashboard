Intelligence Value

Members of Congress are required to disclose stock trades within 45 days under the STOCK Act. These disclosures often reveal positioning ahead of legislation, committee briefings, and classified intelligence briefings. Historically, aggregate Congressional portfolios have outperformed major indices, making these filings a high-value signal for directional conviction. Key metrics include: trade clustering (multiple members trading the same ticker), timing relative to committee assignments, and sector concentration shifts ahead of regulatory action.
