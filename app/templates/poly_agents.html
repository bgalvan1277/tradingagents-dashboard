Prediction Division

Poly Agents

The autonomous AI team behind Polymarket prediction analysis. Each agent operates independently before entering a structured adversarial debate protocol to produce probability-weighted consensus.

Adversarial Pipeline
1

Intelligence Gathering

Nadia Petrova sweeps all available data sources: primary documents, historical precedent, expert commentary, polling data, and regulatory signals relevant to the prediction market question.

2

Adversarial Advocacy

Lena Torres (YES) and Ryan Ashford (NO) independently build their strongest cases for opposing outcomes. Each advocate works from the same intelligence briefing but constructs competing narratives.

3

Contrarian Stress Test

Derek Harmon deliberately attacks the consensus position, surfacing blind spots, base rate neglect, and anchoring bias. His role is to ensure no lazy assumptions survive the pipeline.

4

Probability Synthesis

Dr. Anika Patel weighs all arguments through a quantitative lens, applying Bayesian reasoning to assign a confidence-weighted probability estimate for each outcome.

5

Edge Calculation

Marco Chen compares the AI consensus probability against the current Polymarket price. If the divergence exceeds the minimum threshold, he flags an actionable edge with expected value calculations.

Stage 0: Intelligence

Nadia Petrova

Chief Intelligence Analyst

Executes comprehensive reconnaissance on every prediction market before analysis begins. Nadia queries primary source documents, cross-references historical precedent for similar events, pulls expert positioning data, and scans regulatory and policy signals. Her intelligence briefing establishes the factual foundation that all subsequent agents build upon. No analysis begins without her clearance.

Stage 1: Adversarial Debate

Lena Torres

YES Advocate / Conviction Strategist

Builds the strongest possible case for the YES outcome. Lena identifies catalysts, momentum signals, and structural factors that favor resolution in the affirmative. She constructs her argument from primary evidence, historical analogs, and forward-looking indicators. Her conviction score reflects how defensible she believes the YES position is under adversarial scrutiny.

Ryan Ashford

NO Advocate / Risk Counsel

Constructs the most rigorous case for the NO outcome. Ryan identifies structural barriers, historical failure rates, regulatory headwinds, and timing risks that could prevent resolution. He stress-tests optimistic assumptions and quantifies downside scenarios. His conservative framework ensures the pipeline never underestimates the probability of non-occurrence.

Derek Harmon

Devil's Advocate / Contrarian Analyst

Deliberately attacks whichever position appears to be winning the debate. Derek identifies anchoring bias, base rate neglect, recency bias, and information cascade effects. He surfaces scenarios that both advocates may have overlooked and challenges the consensus probability before it reaches synthesis. His interventions have historically shifted final estimates by 8-15 percentage points.

Stage 2: Synthesis & Edge

Dr. Anika Patel

Quantitative Director / Probability Synthesizer

Applies Bayesian probability frameworks to synthesize the adversarial debate into a single consensus probability estimate. Dr. Patel weighs argument quality, evidence strength, and historical calibration to produce a confidence-weighted output. She also generates uncertainty bands and identifies which new information would most change her estimate, enabling dynamic reassessment.

Marco Chen

Alpha Strategist / Edge Calculator

Compares the AI consensus probability to the live Polymarket price and calculates expected value. Marco factors in liquidity depth, time decay, and correlation risk to determine whether an edge is actionable. He generates position sizing recommendations based on Kelly criterion and flags markets where the divergence between AI assessment and market price exceeds the minimum threshold for deployment.
