How It Works

This platform is built on the TradingAgents framework from Tauric Research, an open-source agentic AI library that orchestrates large language models into specialized analyst roles using a directed graph architecture. Each AI agent operates autonomously within a constrained domain, contributing its independent analysis to a shared state object that flows through the pipeline. No single agent sees the full picture until the final synthesis stage. This is not a chatbot. This is a structured, multi-agent reasoning system where AI models are assigned roles, given context, and forced to produce defensible analysis under adversarial conditions.

The platform has been significantly extended and customized by Brian Galvan, an AI researcher and developer, to incorporate a proprietary intelligence layer that did not exist in the original framework. Specifically, a custom OSINT and reconnaissance engine (Stage 0) was engineered to autonomously sweep 12 public data channels, including SEC regulatory filings, federal contract databases, Congressional trading disclosures, options flow data, insider transactions, macroeconomic indicators, and real-time retail sentiment. This intelligence is compiled into a structured briefing document and injected directly into the agent pipeline, ensuring that every downstream AI analyst, every debate argument, and every risk assessment is grounded in real-world data rather than model hallucination.

The result is a fully autonomous agentic workflow where AI agents independently research, argue, judge, and assess risk before a single investment recommendation is produced. The system mirrors the decision-making structure of an institutional research desk, but operates at machine speed with zero human bias in the analytical chain. The only human in the loop is at the final portfolio decision stage.

The core design principle: adversarial collaboration produces better investment decisions than consensus-seeking. By forcing bull and bear AI advocates to defend opposing theses with evidence from the same dataset, the pipeline surfaces risks and opportunities that a single-model approach would miss entirely.

Stage 2
2

Adversarial Investment Debate

Bull vs. Bear Advocacy System

This is where the system diverges from conventional AI analysis. Two adversarial agents receive the combined output of all four research verticals and are tasked with constructing opposing investment theses. The bull advocate builds the strongest possible case for the position. The bear advocate systematically deconstructs it. Neither agent is allowed to equivocate. They must commit to their position and defend it with evidence.

David Park, Bull Advocate

Identifies asymmetric upside, catalyst timelines, margin-of-safety arguments, and accumulation signals across all research verticals

Catherine Walsh, Bear Advocate

Stress-tests assumptions, models downside scenarios, identifies valuation risks, competitive threats, and earnings quality concerns

Stage 3
3

Research Director Verdict

Michael Torres, Research Director

The Research Director evaluates both sides of the debate without a predetermined bias. His role is judicial: assess the strength of evidence, logical consistency, and analytical rigor of each argument. The verdict determines which thesis is more defensible based on available data. This isn't a popularity contest; the Director's judgment establishes the intellectual framework that informs all subsequent decisions.

Stage 4
4

Risk Assessment Panel

3-Member Risk Evaluation Board

A three-member risk committee examines every position through distinct analytical lenses. This multi-perspective approach ensures that no single risk bias dominates the final assessment. A Risk Judge then synthesizes all three inputs into a unified risk verdict.

Aggressive Analyst

Maximum upside capture potential, optimal entry timing, momentum exploitation windows

Neutral Analyst

Portfolio correlation analysis, concentration risk, position sizing optimization

Conservative Analyst

Worst-case drawdown modeling, capital preservation thresholds, tail risk scenarios

Stage 5
5

Portfolio Manager Consensus

Brian Galvan, Chief Portfolio Manager

All research, analysis, debate outcomes, and risk assessments converge at the portfolio management level. The final investment directive synthesizes the complete output of the multi-agent pipeline. No single agent's recommendation bypasses this final review. Every position change, sizing adjustment, and risk parameter is validated against the complete analytical record before execution.

Final Consensus Output

  • ✓Investment Rating (Strong Buy through Strong Sell, 5-point scale)
  • ✓Confidence Score (weighted aggregate across all agent assessments)
  • ✓Intelligence Briefing (OSINT sweep: 12-channel data compilation)
  • ✓4 Independent Research Reports (Technical, Sentiment, News, Fundamentals)
  • ✓Bull Case Thesis (evidence-backed upside argument)
  • ✓Bear Case Thesis (evidence-backed downside argument)
  • ✓Research Director Verdict (judicial assessment of both theses)
  • ✓Risk Assessment (aggressive, neutral, conservative perspectives)
  • ✓Portfolio Directive (final actionable recommendation with position sizing)
